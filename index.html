Confidential — Released

We wrote the playbook.
Now we're burning it.

For years, we built campaigns that talked people into buying things they didn't need. It worked. That's the problem.

Chaos, Havoc, & More is what happens when the people who knew the tricks stop using them — and start naming them instead. Every project under this name exists to show you exactly how the last fifty years of advertising got you to open your wallet.

Evidence

The old playbook, unredacted.

Hover or tab through each line. These are five of the tactics we used to bill by the hour. They still run today — you've just never seen them named on the page before.

01
Planned obsolescence

Designing products to fail or feel outdated on a schedule, so replacement isn't a choice, it's a deadline.

02
Manufactured scarcity

"Only 3 left" and "ends tonight" — timers and counters designed to override the part of you that would otherwise wait.

03
Subscription creep

One click to join, a phone call and a hold-music apology to leave. The friction is the business model.

04
Astroturfed authenticity

Paid voices dressed up as organic opinion, because a recommendation converts better than an ad ever will.

05
Anchored pricing

A fake "was" price next to the real one, so the discount feels like a win instead of the price it always should've been.
