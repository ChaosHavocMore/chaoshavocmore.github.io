Dossier

Still stuck in 1987. On purpose.

Dr. Phil Ostergaard hasn't updated his opinions, his wardrobe, or his patience since the Reagan administration — because he says the data hasn't changed either. We are a species with a research library and the impulse control of a raccoon near a dumpster. We have the studies. We have the models. We built entire fields of science to predict our own worst behavior, and then we did the behavior anyway.

His thesis, delivered nightly and at volume: humans aren't the smartest species on the planet, we're just the loudest about it. Every decision should run through the data first and the ego second — his own included. He's not exempt. He's not above it. He's a well-spoken Neanderthal with a broadcast license, same as the rest of us, just further along in admitting it.
