Issue 001 · The Mind, Uncensored

The Mind Is A Spectrum, Not A Switch.

We hold space for people living with mental health issues — and we start from a simple premise: a brain is not broken just because a society refuses to change.

Read What We Believe Join The Community

Mission

Hold space. Question the story. Follow the data.

Surfing the Abyss exists for people navigating anxiety, depression, and every diagnosis in between — and for anyone who has ever been told there's something wrong with their brain, full stop, end of conversation. We think that conversation is nowhere near finished.

We are not neurotypical and neurodivergent. We are neurodiverse — one long, wide, human spectrum with no single correct way to think, feel, or process the world. Every mind on it deserves care, not a category.

We also think a lot of what gets filed as "disorder" is a nervous system doing exactly what it evolved to do: responding, hard, to conditions that were never survivable to begin with. That doesn't mean biology is irrelevant. It means biology was never the whole story — and treating it like the whole story has cost people decades of their lives.

Beliefs

What we hold steady, in public, on purpose.

01

General neurodiversity is real. Not "neurotypical vs. neurodivergent" — a spectrum, not a binary switch.

02

Large parts of the DSM-V may describe a sane reaction to an unsane society — and call it a personal defect instead.

03

"Chemical imbalance" is a theory, not a settled fact. Biology explains a piece of the story. It was never the whole book.

04

Anxiety and depression can be adaptive responses to real adversity — not proof that something inside you is malfunctioning.

05

A diagnosis can be a map worth having. It was never meant to be a cage you live in forever.

06

Healing includes context — environment, history, community, and safety — not a prescription pad alone.

Field Report

The data doesn't say what we were told it says.

Researchers across anthropology, psychology, and evolutionary biology have spent decades pushing back on purely biological explanations of mental health — and their case is stronger than the marketing ever let on.

Mismatch

A lot of modern distress looks less like a broken brain and more like an ancient nervous system meeting a world it never evolved for.

Incentives

The industries built around a single biological story have real financial reasons to keep that story simple — and unquestioned.

Stigma

Framing distress as purely biological hasn't reliably reduced stigma. Some research suggests it can quietly make things worse.

Context

Diet, movement, economic security, and belonging all move the needle on mental health — alongside anything a prescription can do.

Community

A place to land, not a place to perform wellness.

We run a space for people to talk about what's actually going on — without having to first prove they deserve support. No toxic positivity, no forced silver linings, no pressure to be "healed" on a schedule.

One honest note: this community is a place to feel less alone and think critically about the systems around mental health — it isn't a replacement for a therapist, doctor, or crisis line. If you're in crisis, please reach out to a local emergency service or crisis line first. We'll be here after.
